The Austin Dam — the predecessor to today's Tom Miller Dam — was
broken for nearly thirty years, washed out in the great floods of
1900. Its reconstruction, completed in 1940, was one of the
signature New Deal projects of central Texas. The lake it created
defined the modern shape of the West Lake area and made the riverside
properties along the western shore newly desirable.
