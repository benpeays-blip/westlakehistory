The Eanes School was the educational anchor of the West Lake community
from the 1870s until the late 1960s, when the district consolidated
into the modern Eanes Independent School District. Children rode
horses, mules, and (later) buses to attend; for decades it was the
only public building in the hills west of the Colorado.

The original schoolhouse no longer stands, but the cemetery and
several family homesteads associated with it survive nearby. The
Eanes History Group is collecting photographs and oral histories from
families connected to the school.
