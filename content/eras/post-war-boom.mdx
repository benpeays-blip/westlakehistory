Between 1945 and 1970 the West Lake area transformed from a sparsely
populated stretch of ranch country into a recognizable suburb. The
Tom Miller Dam, completed in 1940, stabilized Lake Austin and made
the western shoreline newly desirable. Returning veterans — many of
them with VA loans — bought parcels carved out of the old Davenport
and Eanes-area tracts. By the end of the period, Westlake High
School had opened and the modern street grid was largely in place.
